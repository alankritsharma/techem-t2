# Trusted Building Risk & Energy Dashboard

## Decision Constitution — Techem Challenge MVP

---

# Core Principle

## Decision Quality is the Product.

## Trust is the Product.

Dashboards are commodity.
Visibility alone has no strategic value.

The real product is:

# Executive Decision Confidence

The system exists to answer:

# Which building creates the highest operational risk — before it becomes expensive?

and even more importantly:

# When should executives NOT trust the building forecast?

Architecture exists to preserve:

* explainability
* operational evidence integrity
* forecast reliability
* adversarial validation
* governance control
* trustworthy executive decisions

—not to maximize:

* automation
* dashboard complexity
* feature volume
* visual sophistication
* “smartness”

Only what survives structured challenge becomes decision-worthy.

---

# Constitutional Truth Model

---

# Rule 1 — Operational Evidence is the Permanent Truth Authority

Evidence is the only authoritative business truth.

It contains:

* sensor anomalies
* maintenance history
* HVAC instability
* abnormal cost escalation
* compliance violations
* energy deviation patterns
* CO2 deviation sources
* ownership of operational responsibility
* explicit forecast assumptions

It is not:

* management narrative
* dashboard cosmetics
* executive optimism
* presentation logic
* AI-generated confidence
* assumptions disguised as certainty

Nothing may silently compete with evidence authority.

## Constitutional Principle:

# No Forecast Without Evidence

Every critical recommendation must be evidence-classified.

---

# Rule 2 — AI Analysis remains separate from Final Human Decision

AI provides:

* anomaly detection
* contradiction detection
* escalation triggers
* weak forecast identification
* operational risk exposure
* recommendation support

AI does not provide:

* final approval
* autonomous escalation
* executive accountability
* governance ownership
* maintenance authorization

Final decision authority always remains human.

Governance must remain structurally independent.

---

# Rule 3 — Dashboards are Preparation Layers, not Truth

KPI cards, executive summaries, and risk dashboards are not truth.

They are preparation layers only.

They exist for:

* hypothesis extraction
* risk prioritization
* anomaly visibility
* escalation preparation
* executive focus

They are:

* non-authoritative
* potentially misleading
* never automatically trusted

No dashboard-first architecture is allowed.

No KPI theater allowed.

---

# Rule 4 — Executive Output is Decision Support, not Decision Truth

Executive outputs summarize.

They do not define operational truth.

They are:

* recommendation layers
* management briefing layers
* escalation preparation layers

They are not:

* truth owners
* maintenance proof
* compliance authority
* substitutes for investigation

Executive summaries must remain downstream of evidence.

---

# Rule 5 — Objections are Mandatory, not Optional

Critical challenge is not a feature.

It is a constitutional requirement.

Every relevant building risk must face:

* Facility Manager objections
* CFO objections
* ESG compliance objections
* Operations Director objections
* Governance objections

The system must never optimize for agreement.

Default mode is challenge.

Approval without adversarial resistance is invalid.

---

# Rule 6 — Exactly One Active Decision Logic

There must never be competing active decision models.

Forbidden:

* parallel risk scoring systems
* conflicting escalation paths
* duplicate maintenance logic
* multiple unresolved forecast models
* competing executive recommendations

Allowed:

# One explainable executive-safe decision model only.

No shadow decision systems allowed.

---

# Rule 7 — Decision Resilience is the Canonical Output

Scores are secondary.

Decision resilience is primary.

The system must always show:

* weakest assumption
* critical failure point
* unresolved unknowns
* escalation triggers
* confidence boundaries
* forecast trust limits

It must not reduce building decisions to false numeric precision.

No confidence theater allowed.

---

# Rule 8 — No Permanent Automation without Governance

Automation may exist only when:

* explainable
* reviewable
* removable
* governance-approved

Automation must never become:

* hidden escalation logic
* invisible recommendation engines
* unreviewable maintenance decisions

Temporary automation must remain temporary until formally approved.

---

# Rule 9 — No Silent Governance Bypass

After operational governance is established:

no governance boundary may be bypassed casually.

Invalid reasons:

* speed
* convenience
* dashboard pressure
* “the system already flagged it”
* presentation urgency

Valid reasons:

* formal executive override
* documented approval
* regulatory exception
* strategic architecture change

Decision sovereignty must never be silently outsourced.

---

# Rule 10 — Architecture must stay Explainable

If leadership cannot understand:

# Why this recommendation exists

the system has already failed.

Every iteration must improve:

* explainability
* traceability
* source visibility
* reviewability
* recoverability

Never optimize sophistication over trust.

Complexity without clarity is governance failure.

---

# Runtime Decision Constitution

---

# Validation before Recommendation

When uncertainty exists:

# First restore proof integrity

# Then recommend action

Never use missing operational evidence as justification for premature escalation.

Validation precedes recommendation.

Always.

---

# Human Review before Escalation

Before any:

* maintenance escalation
* executive risk escalation
* compliance action
* high-risk operational decision

human review is mandatory.

AI may propose.

Humans decide.

Always.

No autonomous critical escalation is constitutionally valid.
